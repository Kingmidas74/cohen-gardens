One-way road

November 1952, San Francisco
Alcatraz Federal Penitentiary

Today he heard these phrases everywhere. Rejoiced mostly Republicans, of course. Although he himself did not particularly share the strange joy of his colleagues and friends. The event is quite ordinary. But many believed that this was a new chapter in almost the history of the entire United States. Everything else was familiar. Briefing, armory, posts, shift every four hours. Sometimes Barney caught himself thinking that he was a little tired of this routine. So today there was at least some variety.
